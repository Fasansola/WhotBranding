01 · Background

What WhotFrenzy Is

Just enough to design well. Here's the gist of the product.

WhotFrenzy is a serious, competitive mobile platform built around Whot, the classic Nigerian card game millions already play. It's not a casual card game and it's not a gambling app. It's a skill competition: players go head to head in fast 1v1 matches, climb leaderboards, win real prizes, and build lasting status as champions.

It's a nightly thing. There's a tournament every evening that crowns a champion and celebrates the best players. There's a strong community and "best player you know" energy to it too. We're launching in Nigeria first.

The shorthand in my head: WhotFrenzy is competitive energy with Nigerian cultural heat. Think a serious competition or e-sports brand that grew up in Lagos and actually plays Whot.
NAME STORY
One bit of background that might be useful, not as something to spell out anywhere, just to keep in the back of your mind for the feeling. The name plays on "Whot and Friends." Underneath all the competition, that's really what it's about: people playing the game they grew up on, with the people they know. The brand can be fierce and competitive on the surface, but there's warmth and community sitting right under it. If that spirit quietly comes through somewhere, great.

02 · Direction

The Feeling & What to Avoid

The vibe I'm chasing, and a few traps I really want us to dodge.

The feeling

Competitive fire. Confidence. Warm, but not soft. Opening the app should feel like stepping into an arena, not opening a utility. It takes the player seriously, celebrates skill, and always points forward. I want it to look like it belongs next to the best competition brands anywhere, while feeling unmistakably Nigerian.

What I really don't want it to look like

×
Not a casino. No spinning wheels, coin stacks, "WIN!" banners, dice, nothing that reads as gambling. This is a skill competition and the look has to back that up.
×
Not a casual card game. No wood-table backgrounds, rounded playful fonts, or bright primary-colour palettes. The existing Nigerian Whot apps look like that, and I want us to look like what they wish they could be.
×
Not a fintech app. Clean and corporate kills the energy. People should feel a bit of heat when they open it, not the calm of a banking app.
×
Not generically "African-themed." No Ankara patterns, no map-of-Africa outlines, no surface-level cultural decoration. The Nigerian identity should come through in energy and attitude, not decoration pasted on top.
HOW WE'LL WORK
You drive. I'd just love to see things as they develop, early directions, rough concepts, whatever, so I can give quick reactions and we don't go too far down a path before checking in. Otherwise, your process is your process.

07 · Asset

Whot Cards

The cards are the product. Premium, and readable at speed.

Whot is played with a 50-card deck across five shapes (Circle, Triangle, Cross, Square and Star), plus the special Whot card. The big thing to know: during a match, cards have to be readable at a glance, fast. So they need to look premium and distinctive, but never so busy that the shape and number get hard to read mid-game. Here's the set I need:

The card treatments

Normal card
One consistent look for standard number cards across all five shapes.
Special card
A distinct look for the action cards, so players can tell at a glance a card is special. One special-card style.
The Whot card (20)
The wild card, numbered 20, played to call a shape. It's the single most special card in the deck and should have its own standout design, clearly above everything else.
Card back
The face-down back. It's on screen constantly, so it needs to look great and feel like the brand.
One real ask: please don't take inspiration from existing Whot apps or other card-game apps. I'd love this to feel like ours, not borrowed.

08 · Asset

Skill Badges

Four badges, one family. They're proof of achievement players show off.

Badges live on a player's profile as proof of what they've pulled off, basically a trophy room. I need four for now. They should feel like one family (a shared look) while each clearly stands for its own thing. What each one looks like is entirely your creative call, I'm just giving you the name and what it means.

The four

Chain God
For the longest chain of cards played back to back without the opponent getting a turn. Think: an unbroken chain, momentum, a streak.
Unbeatable
For the longest run of consecutive wins. Think: dominance, an unbroken winning streak.
The Machine
For relentless, efficient, fast play across a full session. Think: precision, a machine that doesn't stop.
Invincible
The big one. A perfect session, every game won, zero losses. The rarest, most prestigious. It should clearly look a tier above the other three.
ONE THING
For Invincible, if the badge shows a number, build that number as its own swappable element rather than baking it into the art. The target shifts over time, so I want to be able to change the number without redrawing the whole badge.

09 · Application

Social Graphics — Five

Two fixed hero pieces and three reusable templates I'll fill again and again.
Before the list, the thing I care about most here. How these look is hugely important to me. Hugely. I can't overstate it. They have to look genuinely amazing, the kind of work that makes someone stop scrolling. But looking amazing can't be the whole job. A lot of beautiful designs, the kind that fill designers' portfolios, don't actually do anything: they don't pull the eye to the point and they don't get anyone to take an action. So I need both: stunning to look at and built to convert. Each one has a single job (get a sign-up, get a share, make someone want in), and the design has to drive the viewer toward that action. Beautiful and effective, not one or the other.

Three of these are templates, a designed shell where only the name, photo, stat or amount changes, because we'll be posting fresh results often. Please design for the main platforms (Instagram, X, WhatsApp status, Facebook); plan for square and vertical/story formats and we'll confirm exact sizes.

Used often
Template

2

Sign-up driver
Hero

Our launch headline, built to get people to sign up. The whole job of this one is conversion: make the offer impossible to scroll past and obvious what to do next.

Invincible Promo
The dream
Hero

This is the aspirational one. A perfect session, win every game, lose none, for the biggest prize on the platform. It is the "wait, is that even possible?" graphic that makes people want to try. I want this built as a reusable template, not a one-off, and here is the honest reason why. The target for Invincible is going to change as we grow. It starts at one number of wins and moves up later, and the prize figure could move too. If those numbers are baked into the artwork, every change means paying for a fresh design. If the win count and the amount are their own swappable layers in a clean template, I just drop in the new number myself and the graphic keeps working for years. So please treat the figures as editable fields sitting on top of a design that stays the same.
